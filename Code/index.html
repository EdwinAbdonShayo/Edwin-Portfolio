My Projects & Activities

Mauritius Internet Governance Forum

During my time at Halley Movement, I collaborated with the team to organize the Mauritius Internet Governance Forum (Mauritius IGF) for 2023, part of the United Nations Initiatives. Additionally, during the main event, I served as one of the panelists and presented on the future of Artificial Intelligence (AI), specifically focusing on Model Languages. I elaborated on the rapid development of AI and the future prospects of Prompt Engineering. Furthermore, I addressed the potential risks associated with AI and emphasized that with proper measures in place, we can look forward to a brighter future.

The LEEM's Regulator

This device was designed to save electricity and protect devices from damage caused by being left plugged in for extended periods. An Arduino Nano microcontroller was used to control a relay, turning it on and off based on a set timer.

Graphics & Video Editing

This project involved creating a lyrics video for "Njoo Tuparty". I designed graphics with Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop, animated with After Effects and Premiere Pro, and assembled the video using Wondershare Filmora.

Front & Back End Solutions

This project included developing a web application with a front-end in HTML5 and CSS3. JavaScript was used for the back-end to connect to a MongoDB database holding login details.

School of Internet Governance 2024

In early 2024, as part of the Halley Movement team, I helped organize the month-long School of Internet Governance (SIG) event. It began with an opening session and continued with virtual sessions, where I served as the moderator. The Mauritius SIG aimed to educate participants on leveraging the internet effectively while being aware of its dangers.
